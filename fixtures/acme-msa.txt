MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date by and between Meridian Cloud Services, Inc., a Delaware corporation with offices at 500 Harbor Boulevard, San Mateo, California ("Provider"), and the customer identified in the applicable Order Form ("Customer"). Provider and Customer are each a "Party" and together the "Parties." This Agreement governs Provider's delivery of hosted software, professional services, and related support (collectively, the "Services").

1. Term and Automatic Renewal. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of twenty-four (24) months. Thereafter it shall automatically renew for successive twelve-month periods on an evergreen basis, and each such renewal shall occur automatically unless a Party delivers written notice of non-renewal no later than ninety (90) days before the end of the then-current term. Fees for each renewal term may increase by up to eight percent (8%) without Customer's consent.

2. Fees, Payment, and Late Charges. Customer shall pay all fees stated in each Order Form within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. Any late payment shall accrue interest at one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower. All fees are non-cancelable and all amounts paid are non-refundable, and Provider may suspend the Services on any past due balance.

3. Limitation of Liability. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, the Customer's aggregate liability arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be uncapped and unlimited, and shall not be subject to any limitation as to amount or type, including liability for indirect, incidental, consequential, and punitive damages. Provider's total liability, by contrast, shall in no event exceed the fees paid by Customer in the one (1) month preceding the claim. This uncapped exposure applies even where the loss was reasonably avoidable by Provider.

4. Indemnification. Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Provider and its affiliates, officers, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to Customer's use of the Services, without regard to the underlying cause. This indemnification obligation is broad, applies to claims of every kind, and survives termination of this Agreement.

5. Confidentiality and Data Protection. Each Party shall protect the other's Confidential Information using no less than a reasonable degree of care. Customer appoints Provider as a data processor and authorizes Provider to process personal data on Customer's behalf under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other applicable data protection laws. Provider may transfer and process personal data in any jurisdiction, including outside the European Economic Area, and may engage sub-processors without Customer's prior specific authorization.

6. Protected Health Information. Where Customer transmits protected health information (PHI) through the Services, Customer designates Provider as a business associate under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). Provider shall apply commercially reasonable safeguards to such protected health information, but Provider's duties with respect to PHI are limited to those expressly stated in this Section, and Provider disclaims all other obligations concerning health data.

7. Intellectual Property; Work Made for Hire. All deliverables, feedback, configurations, and materials arising from the Services shall be deemed work made for hire, and to the extent any such materials do not so qualify, Customer irrevocably assigns to Provider all right, title, and interest, including all intellectual property rights, in any suggestions, ideas, or improvements Customer provides. Customer retains no ownership in derivative works created by Provider.

8. Non-Competition and Non-Solicitation. During the term and for two (2) years thereafter, Customer shall not, directly or indirectly, engage or invest in any business that competes with Provider, and shall not solicit for employment any employee or contractor of Provider. Customer acknowledges that these non-compete and non-solicitation covenants are reasonable and necessary to protect Provider's legitimate interests.

9. Dispute Resolution; Arbitration and Class Action Waiver. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively by binding, confidential arbitration before a single arbitrator. Each Party waives any right to a trial by jury and waives any right to bring or participate in a class action, collective action, or class arbitration (the "Class Action Waiver"). Injunctive relief in aid of arbitration may be sought only by Provider.

10. Warranties and Disclaimers. Provider warrants only that the Services will materially conform to its then-current published documentation. Except as expressly stated in this Section, the Services are provided "as is," and Provider disclaims all other warranties, whether express or implied, including the implied warranties of merchantability and fitness for a particular purpose.

11. Termination. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for a material breach that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice. Upon any termination, all fees owed through the end of the then-current term become immediately due and payable, and Customer shall have no right to any refund of prepaid fees.

12. Governing Law and Venue. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles, and the Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware.

13. Notices and Entire Agreement. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses stated in the applicable Order Form. This Agreement, together with all Order Forms, is the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous understandings, and no modification is effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.
